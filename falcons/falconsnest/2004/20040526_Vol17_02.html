Falcons Smack Smith Barney 8-1
By Larry Spozdzial / Falcons Online
May 26, 2004

The Falcons playing their second game of this young season (the 26th!) at chilly damp Diamond 5 North Meadow got their hitting and pitching act together and came away with an easy 8-1 victory over one of the Falcons' more prominent rivals Smith Barney Wednesday night. The victory evened the team's record at 1 and 1.

The scoring for the Falcons began immediately in the bottom of the first as Adam Bloom led off with a single followed by singles from Pat Casserly and Bob Rupe for 1 run, Aaron Scheid then reached on a fielder's choice and Vernon Chin delivered a RBI single scoring Pat and after RJ Filipski singled sending Aaron to third, Scott Miller's sac fly scored Aaron for the early 3-0 lead.

Things were quiet for both teams especially with Bob Kostulias pitching and fielding well, the latter coming into play when the Bullet stabbed a liner from Barney's leadoff hitter in the top of the 4th, until the bottom of the 5th when the Falcons picked up 5 more runs to ice the win. After Bob K struck out to start the inning and Adam Bloom tripled to right, Pat C. fouled out, there followed consecutive singles by Bob Rupe and Aaron Scheid, Vernon reached and consecutive singles from RJ, Scott and a 2 run single off the bat of Eric Sandurs made for the 8-0 lead.

As it turned out that's all the Falcons needed to win as Smith Barney couldn't produce any hitting to speak of and combined that with poor base running made things easy for the Falcons. However the Barney gang managed a run in the 7th on a single, a force and a triple and the final score became the aforementioned 8-1 Falcons.
